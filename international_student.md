Language is a commonly reported barrier to adjusting to U.S. university life, followed by financial difficulties and problems adapting to the culture.  

* [Academic Writing Support for International Graduate Students](https://eli.ufl.edu/academic-written-english/): The Scholarly Writing Course is available to UF Graduate Students, Post-Docs, or Visiting Scholars. This online-only class meets synchronously for 1.5 hours per week. An additional 1.5 hours of instruction will be provided asynchronously and with one-on-one feedback from the instructor. For information, contact Jen Ramos.
* [University Writing Program](https://writing.ufl.edu/resources/for-students/): If you are an English as a Second Language (ESL) student, you may visit the Writing Studio to improve your writing skills. By working one-on-one with a writing tutor, you can receive assistance brainstorming, drafting, and revising your writing. In addition to answering questions about basic form and usage when writing in English, tutors can also provide support for larger writing projects such as thesis and dissertation chapters. Please contact the office at 352-846-1138.

Financial strain is difficult to navigate as the students were required to show proof of funds to secure the visa. This means funding is very limited in the form of scholarships, for example. However, UF has the Hitchcock Field & Fork Pantry to help with basic needs.

* [The Pantry](https://pantry.fieldandfork.ufl.edu/about/visit/) is a free resource for members of the UF community to access supplementary food staples such as bread, nonperishable canned items, frozen meat, and seasonal produce from the Field & Fork Farm and Gardens.

Even though international students are subject to the same stresses of academic and personal life as their US counterparts, these stresses are compounded by being in an unfamiliar culture. 

* [The CWC’s clinical services](https://counseling.ufl.edu/resources/international/) are available for any current UF student. We offer Short-Term Individual Counseling, Group Counseling, and Couples Counseling. Follow the link to learn more about these services and call us at (352) 392-1575 for a brief consultation.

Finally, the International Center is working on a YouTube playlist for helping those students who will move to the US for the first time. Your department is welcome to view and share these videos to learn more about things students commonly want information about pre-arrival.

* [The UFIC International Students & Scholars YouTube playlist](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9Sx3J5MgHF8&list=PLLHGrbvcNja6UmIwCVmULHTcZzfyE0VJX) has information on immunizations, health care in the US, off campus housing, and taxes.
